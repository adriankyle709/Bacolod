Welcome to Bacolod

Nestled in the heart of Lanao del Norte, Bacolod is a charming municipality that offers a unique blend of natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and warm Filipino hospitality. Discover the authentic Mindanao experience in this peaceful community.

Population

The municipality of Bacolod, Lanao del Norte, had a population of 24,367 as of the 2020 census. While there may be slight population fluctuations, this number represents the most recent official count.

Land Area

The municipality has a land area of 104.10 square kilometers or 40.19 square miles which constitutes 3.10% of Lanao del Norte's total area.

Founding Date

Established on May 10, 1956, when it was officially recognized as a municipality of Lanao del Norte province.

A Model of Harmony: Muslim and Christian Unity

Bacolod, Lanao del Norte stands as a shining example of peaceful coexistence between Muslim and Christian communities. For generations, these two faiths have lived side by side in mutual respect and understanding.

The municipality celebrates this unity through:

  • Interfaith dialogues and community gatherings
  • Joint celebrations of religious holidays like Eid'l Fitr and Christmas
  • Shared participation in local festivals and civic activities
  • Collaborative peace-building initiatives
  • Mixed neighborhoods where families of different faiths live as good neighbors

Local Delicacies of Bacolod

Bacolod offers a delightful array of traditional foods that reflect its cultural diversity and agricultural abundance.

Pater

Steamed rice topped with flavorful shredded chicken or beef, wrapped in banana leaves - a popular Maranao breakfast.

Dodol

A sweet, sticky treat made from coconut milk, brown sugar, and rice flour, often served during special occasions.

Bibingka

Bibingka is a traditional Filipino rice cake that's especially popular during Christmas, fiestas, and other celebrations.

Puto Maya

Steamed sticky rice with coconut milk, typically served with ripe mangoes and hot chocolate.

Where to Find These Delicacies

  • • Bacolod Public Market - Morning and Evening food stalls
  • • Local fiestas and celebrations
  • • Home-based businesses in various barangays

Key Ingredients

  • • Fresh coconut from local plantations
  • • Organic rice from Bacolod's fields
  • • Native chicken and beef
  • • Locally grown spices and herbs

Municipal Mission & Vision

Mission

To provide quality services to the people of Bacolod through transparent governance, sustainable development programs, and community empowerment, ensuring a progressive and peaceful municipality for all residents.

Vision

A progressive, resilient, and united Bacolod where empowered citizens enjoy quality services, sustainable livelihood opportunities, and a peaceful community anchored on good governance and cultural preservation.

Cultural Highlights

Bacolod boasts a rich tapestry of cultural traditions that reflect its diverse heritage and historical influences.

Local Traditions

Experience the Sagayan Festival, celebrating ancestral traditions through music, dance, and colorful traditional attire that honors Bacolod's rich cultural heritage.

Religious Practices

Witness the harmonious coexistence of Catholic and Islamic traditions, with beautiful mosques and churches serving as centers for spiritual gatherings and celebrations.

Indigenous Culture

Discover the rich heritage of local indigenous communities through their intricate weaving, traditional medicine practices, and oral storytelling traditions.

Community Events

Join the lively Araw ng Bacolod celebrations, featuring parades, sports competitions, cultural performances, and community feasts that bring together residents of all ages.

Geographic Features

Situated in the northern region of Mindanao, Bacolod offers diverse landscapes and strategic connectivity to major cities.

Notable Landmarks

  • Bacolod Municipal Hall - government center
  • Santo Niño parish - Religious landmark
  • Pagayawan falls - Natural attraction
  • Masidlakon falls - Natural attraction
  • Buaknabato falls - Natural attraction
  • Pasi-Taytayan - Sunset view

Connectivity

  • 2.5 hours from Cagayan de Oro
